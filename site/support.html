Questions, answers, and where to report problems

Cadence is in active development. Everything routes through GitHub — issues are read, and bug reports genuinely shape what gets fixed first.

Report a bug

Something crashed, failed, or behaved wrong. Include your Windows version, GPU, and what you were doing — screenshots help.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Does Cadence need an internet connection?
Only for the initial downloads — the app, the models, and dependencies. After that, generation and voice training run entirely offline. The exceptions are optional cloud providers (Claude for lyrics, Suno or ElevenLabs for generation), which you have to deliberately enable.
Is my voice uploaded anywhere?
No. Recordings are saved as WAV files on your machine, the voice model is trained on your machine, and both stay there. Cadence has no server, no account system, and no telemetry.
Do I need a GPU?
An NVIDIA GPU with 8 GB of VRAM or more is strongly recommended — generation takes seconds on a modern card. Everything can fall back to CPU, but expect generation to take minutes rather than seconds.
Which languages are supported?
Prompts and lyrics work in more than fifty languages, including mixed-language requests — for example, a prompt in English asking for lyrics in Malayalam. Quality is strongest in widely spoken languages and improves with a Claude API key for the lyric-writing step.
Do I need any API keys?
No. The default setup is fully local: Ollama handles lyric writing and ACE-Step handles music, both free and on your machine. A Claude key is an optional upgrade for better multilingual lyrics; Suno and ElevenLabs keys are optional alternatives for generation.
How much recording does voice training need?
Ten to fifteen minutes of clean speech, captured in short takes with a guided reading script inside the app. You review each take and re-record anything that isn't right before training starts.
Where are my tracks saved?
As WAV files in a local folder, with an MP3 export option. They appear in the app's Library, but they're ordinary files — you can copy, move, or back them up like anything else on your disk.
When can I just download an installer?
Phase 7 on the roadmap ships a Windows .msi installer. macOS and Linux builds follow in Phase 8, after Windows is solid.
